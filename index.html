SegTalker: Segmentation-based Talking Face Generation with Mask-guided Fine-grained Attribute Editing

Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Given a talking video and another audio, SegTalker can produce high-fidelity and synchronized video with rich texture details (row 2), enabling swapping background (row 3) and facial editing such as blinking (row 4).

Abstract

Audio-driven Talking Face Generation aims to synthesize video with lip movements synchronized to input speech. However, current generative techniques face challenges in preserving intricate regional textural details (skin, teeth). To address the aforementioned challenges, we propose a novel framework called \textbf{SegTalker} to decouple lip movements and image texture details by introducing segmentation as intermediate representation. More specifically, given the mask employed by a parser network, we first leverage the speech to drive the mask and generate talking segmentation. Then we disentangle semantic regions into style codes using a mask-guided encoder. Ultimately, we inject the previously generated talking segmentations and style codes into a mask-guided StyleGAN to synthesize video frames. In this way, mostly of texture details are fully preserved. Moreover, our approach can inherently achieve background separation and facilitate mask-guided facial attribute editing. In particular, by editing the local areas of mask (e.g. eyes, eyebrows), and feeding the modified segmentation along with style codes into the StyleGAN, our approach enables localized face editing. Experiments demonstrate that our proposed approach can effectively preserve texture details while remaining competitive in lip synchronization. Quantitative results on the HDTF dataset illustrate the superior performance of our method over existing methods on most metrics.
